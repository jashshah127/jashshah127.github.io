Mission:Brain Foundation Hackathon · Global Health · AI

S.L.A.N.T — Sierra Leone Assessment for Neurotrauma Triage

Ranked 9th out of 62 teams at the Mission:Brain Foundation Hackathon (2024). The challenge: Sierra Leone has just 97 ambulances and 1 referral hospital serving 8 million people across 27,700 sq miles. We had to design a triage system deployable by ambulance dispatch at Sierra Leone's NEMS to prioritize neurotrauma cases under extreme resource constraints.

Built SLANT — a semi-automatic neurotrauma triage system that scores patients across 6 clinical parameters (Glasgow Coma Scale, vital signs, pupil reactivity, pain response, motor strength, spinal cord injury) to assign priority levels (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and trigger real-time ambulance dispatch notifications. Designed for offline-first use with a bilingual UI (English + Krio) to serve first responders in remote areas with no internet connectivity.

  • Designed and coded the full triage algorithm in Python — scoring engine, priority logic, patient tracking, and ambulance dispatch simulation
  • Architected offline-first functionality: local data storage with sync on connectivity restore
  • Developed go-to-market strategy targeting NEMS, government health agencies, and NGOs with a nonprofit foundation model
  • Ranked 9th of 62 competing teams from across global health, engineering, and medical disciplines
